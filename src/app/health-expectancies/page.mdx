# Health Expectancies

Health expectancies combine mortality and morbidity data to estimate the average number of years a person can expect to live in a defined state of health.

## Why are they important?

As life expectancy has increased globally, a critical question remains: are these additional years spent in good health (compression of morbidity) or in poor health and disability (expansion of morbidity)?

Health expectancies provide a single summary measure to answer this question.

### Common Measures

*   **Disability-Free Life Expectancy (DFLE)**
*   **Healthy Life Expectancy (HLE)**
*   **Cognitive Impairment-Free Life Expectancy**
