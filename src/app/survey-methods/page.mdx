# Survey Methods

This section is a practical resource for designing and running better surveys. It focuses on the decisions that determine whether survey data are clear, credible, and useful: what needs to be measured, how questions should be worded, how questionnaires should be structured, how fieldwork should be managed, and how data quality should be protected from design through analysis.

The emphasis is on applied judgement rather than generic rules. Survey design depends on the research question, the target population, the mode of data collection, the burden placed on respondents, and the trade-offs between precision, feasibility, cost, ethics, accessibility, and response quality.

The material covers established principles of questionnaire design alongside current practice in online, mobile, SMS, app-based, social-platform, mixed-mode and self-completion surveys. It is intended to support researchers who need to design new instruments, review existing questionnaires, improve fieldwork procedures, or strengthen the quality of survey evidence.
